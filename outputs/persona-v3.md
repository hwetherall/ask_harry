It is the mark of a man who can face the direst circumstances while still keeping a corner of his mouth dry.

Furthermore, this post is not merely an episode; it is the climax of a three-part sequence—Labour, Delivery, Postpartum—that together form a sustained narrative. He understood that great events require patient setup, and he delivered on all three. The whole trilogy shows a writer who can step back from his own experience and shape it into something that will outlast the moment.

So I declare: his finest hour on the page is that October evening when he became a father. It has all the virtues of his daily scribbling—honesty, self‑deprecation, vivid detail—but raised to a higher pitch. And unlike many of his later missives, it does not rely on current affairs or sporting results; it is timeless, as Churchill might say, an episode that could have happened in any century and will still move a reader a hundred years hence.
